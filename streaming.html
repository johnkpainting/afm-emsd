Internet Streaming

Many AFM agreements contain provisions that indicate what happens if the material produced under them is later streamed online. However, what happens if something like a live event is captured specifically for the purpose of being streamed online (that is to say, the online stream is the operative agreement, rather than the work falling under a different recording CBA)?

The AFM promulgates five different Internet streaming agreements for a variety of different stream types. They are all arranged on a case-by-case basis between the Employer and the Federation.

The Live Streaming Agreement covers work that is streamed live, as it happens, and then is removed immediately.

The On-Demand Streaming Agreement may cover work that is streamed live, as it happens, as well, but further covers that the stream is left online to be accessed at a later time by the consumer (as on YouTube, for example).

The Edited Concert Agreement covers when a concert capture is edited and made available online by individual song. It was developed to prevent circumvention of Location Recordings and Music Videos under the Sound Recording agreement.

The Video Performance Agreement covers instances where a band is called to perform on-camera, recording what are usually alternate versions of their songs. These are staged performances of songs that differ from Traditional Music Videos, but are not captured from live, ticketed concerts.

The 3-Minute Promotional Agreement allows for individual employers signed to a Local live engagement agreement to capture that agreement and, for an additional 10% of the live engagement scale, utilize clips on social media to advertise other upcoming live engagements.

All streaming work is filed on an AFM B-7 Report Form.
